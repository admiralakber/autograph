Keep a creature and it becomes a node in a Merkle-DAG lineage: its id is the hash of its content including its parents' ids, signed with your key (Web Crypto, ECDSA P-256). Export it, send it to a friend, re-import it — every hash and signature is re-checked. No chain. No token.

    🛡️ What's real here, and what's illustrative

    • Real: a genuinely-evolving CPPN, real MAP-Elites quality-diversity, the deterministic seed→creature ritual, and a real signed, hash-chained lineage you can export and verify (Web Crypto).
    • Real, but approximate: the self-encoding loop closes to a tolerance, never bit-exactly — this is the honest, single-device cousin of Chang & Lipson's neural-network quine. The loop fidelity you see is measured live, never faked.
    • Watch out for the trivial fixed point: a blank, near-flat creature "encodes itself" perfectly — and says nothing. That's why we showcase the lively ones: structured pictures that still re-state their own weights. Self-reference only matters when it is load-bearing.
    • Illustrative / roadmap: the worldwide swarm (this runs only on your device), zkML "proof of becoming", and the quantum framing — narrative and lineage, never a claim. There are no qubits here.

    Grounding

    The maths, in thirty seconds

    Self-reference is a fixed point

    Kleene's recursion theorem guarantees programs that can read their own description exist; Gödel's incompleteness is the same diagonal trick aimed at provability. A quine is just a fixed point of "run me".

    Replication is that trick, made physical

    Von Neumann's universal constructor uses a description twice: decoded (built) and copied (inherited) — anticipating DNA. Your CPPN genome is exactly that genotype/phenotype split.

    And it's been built in a net

    Chang & Lipson's neural-network quine (2018) trains a network to output its own weights via the HyperNEAT coordinate→weight trick. Autograph evolves the picture version of that idea.

    A lovely bonus: a 2025 result finds that open-endedly evolved networks develop cleaner, more factored internal structure than ones hammered into shape by gradient descent — so a crowd-grown self-portrait may be more legible than what a datacentre would produce. Open-endedness, a 2024 position paper argues, may be essential to the next era of AI.

    Pillar I · the cryptographic mirror 🔐

    A creature that can prove itself

    Self-drawing is one step from self-certifying. The interest here is cryptography-as-mathematics — hashes, commitments, signatures, proofs — never coins. Every idea below stands without a token.

    A signed tree of life

    A phylogeny is a DAG (crossover has two parents). Make it a signed, content-addressed Merkle-DAG: every genome's id is the hash of its weights, its parents' ids, its seed and its fitness, signed with the author's key. You get tamper-evident ancestry, attribution and anti-fraud — the way Git and Certificate Transparency do it, with no chain.

    ✅ real, in the demo above

    Your key grows your creature

    A public key is just high-entropy bytes; feed it through a deterministic generator and the same key always grows the same creature — a publicly checkable witness to the key. This is the Art Blocks / fxhash "hash seeds a deterministic generator" pattern — the determinism does the work, not a marketplace.

    🔭 north star · research-grade, not built

    Proof of becoming (zkML)

    The dream: each elite carries a succinct zero-knowledge proof that it truly achieved its fitness — verified, not re-run. Our nets are tiny, where zkML's prover/verifier asymmetry is friendliest (~5 KB proofs, ~1 s to verify). Folding the whole history into one recursive proof (à la Mina) is the horizon — proving cost is the gate, so we name it a telescope, not a feature.

    🚩 Anti-grift red line: no token, no manufactured scarcity, no "buy in to participate". If a feature only makes sense with a coin attached, it isn't in Autograph.

    Pillar II · the soul's physics ⚛️

    A creature that cannot be cloned — only re-grown

    This is the one place the poetry is, word for word, a law of physics.

    The no-cloning theorem (Wootters & Zurek, 1982) forbids photocopying an arbitrary unknown quantum state. Naïvely that kills self-replication — until you notice that replication was never about cloning the live thing. Von Neumann's constructor passes on a description (copied) and regrows the body (built); life does the same with DNA; Marletto (2015) shows this is fully compatible with quantum theory. The prohibition is the gift: the impossibility of copying is exactly what makes reproduction real rather than mere duplication — and, à la Breuer (1995), a creature can pass on its recipe yet never perfectly measure itself. Gödel even shows up in real physics — the spectral gap is undecidable.

    ⚛️ Honest quantum note: there are no qubits here. Quantum mechanics is our metaphor and our lineage, not our runtime. We claim no quantum speedup — none exists for this embarrassingly-parallel, classical workload. The qubits stay in the museum.

    Lend a hand 🙋

    How to grow it further

    Today, Autograph runs entirely on your one device — no backend, no swarm, no telemetry. The crowd is the roadmap. Here's the honest map of now and next.

    Fork the engine

    It's open source and dependency-light: a real CPPN, MAP-Elites, a WGSL render core with a Canvas fallback, and a Web-Crypto lineage. Clone it, break it, make it yours.

    The swarm (roadmap)

    One WGSL core, WebGPU now Baseline, evaluated identically from a phone to a headless H100; volunteer results made trustworthy with BOINC-style replication. Opt-in, visible, revocable — never stealth mining.

    The Atlas of Self-Reference

    Every discovered kind of strange loop, published as an open, browsable dataset with lineage — free for artists, teachers, mathematicians and the merely curious.

    🔋 Honest energy note: per watt, datacentres win. A browser swarm's value is harvesting already-powered, idle hardware for loss-tolerant quality-diversity search at ~zero marginal cost — not efficiency, and emphatically not training frontier models.
