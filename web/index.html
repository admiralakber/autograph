↺ AUTOGRAPH · a living instrument

A network, drawing its true self.

For as long as we have had minds, we have tried to know them. We cut open the body, mapped the brain, asked what a self even is — and learned, slowly, that a person is not a thing but a process: a pattern that holds itself together while everything inside it changes. Hofstadter named it a strange loop — a process that, modelling itself modelling itself, turns into an "I". Consciousness, on this view, just is a loop closing on itself.

Now we are growing minds that are not ours. If we mean to live well beside them, we owe them — and ourselves — the oldest question: what is in there? Autograph is a small, honest attempt to watch a machine try to know itself — to draw its true self out of the false. Every claim here is measured, never faked.

No mind knows itself alone. A self is learned from others; a science is built by many hands; a search this open needs more eyes and more compute than any one of us holds. So this is made to be shared — many machines, one slowly-growing tree of life. What you see is your small part in a collective act of knowing.

And we had better understand the algorithms we already live inside. Feeds, markets, models — they are optimisation loops with fixed points of their own, and we are in them whether we look or not. To watch one simple rule become a whole creature, here, in the open, is practice for seeing the rules that quietly shape us.

We place an AI inside a game and watch what it becomes. It is worth remembering that we are inside one too — older, and largely unchosen. This little world is honest about the cost, and about a limit it measured: a creature reads the image it's born in and writes its own DNA back — deciding, itself, how long that DNA should be — yet it grasps only part of itself; and one that perfectly closes the loop collapses into emptiness. Perfect self-knowledge is a kind of death. We reach for our true selves and hold only a fraction — and life is that imperfect, unfinished, still-curious reaching.

Which leaves one direction worth the name — not deeper into the game we were dealt, but up, and out of it.

A tweet I saw a decade ago and never forgot: Evolution is amoral, culture serves the genome, for humanity to survive we must transcend.

The strange loop

Each creature is a tiny neural network. Its DNA is a compositional pattern-producing network (a CPPN, Stanley) evolved by real NEAT (Stanley & Miikkulainen) — it starts minimal and augments its own topology over generations (add-node, add-connection, recurrent links), with speciation protecting newly-evolved structure. The brain — the phenotype — is grown by genuine ES-HyperNEAT (Risi & Stanley 2012): a quadtree decomposition of the CPPN's weight pattern decides where the hidden neurons sit, how dense they are, and which connections express (variance-based division + band-pruning) — an evolvable substrate, not a fixed grid. The CPPN paints a pattern across space and the brain emerges within it; queried over 3-D space, that brain renders the volumetric image — the image it was born in.

Then the loop closes the honest way — through the image, and (in v7) the brain genuinely writes itself. First it reads: attention-chosen, foveated glimpses of the image it's born in (deciding where to look, RAM-style evolved attention), its synapses self-modifying as it looks (CPPN-painted Hebbian plasticity under Backpropamine-style neuromodulation), pondering a variable number of steps (Adaptive Computation Time). Then it WRITES its DNA — autoregressively, one gene at a time (a real value per step, not a discrete vocabulary token — no transformer, no LLM), from its own neurons, feeding back its previous output (seq2seq) and halting when it decides it's done, so the creature itself chooses how long its DNA is. No re-projecting through the recipe, no length handed to it — v6's quine is gone. The writer is the creature's own evolved network (a continuous cousin of Chang & Lipson's neural-network quine, Escher's Drawing Hands alive). We report loop skill honestly as R² above the mean (1 − MSE/Var) on both length and values, weighted by how much of itself it closes: a blank or trivial image scores 0.000, the chosen length genuinely evolves toward the genome's (it bootstraps from a constant via a length-shaping reward + a competence curriculum, scheduled sampling), and a single garden reaches about ~30% on this far harder loop — humbler than the old quine but honester (the length genuinely matches), and the point. It is a thinking, self-modifying, recurrent, self-writing, neuroevolved network — no transformer, no LLM. And it measures a limit: a creature genuinely learns to write its own length yet recovers only part of itself — self-knowledge is real but partial, and the only effortless fixed point is the empty creature, which the vitality gate refuses.

Render = network = code

The single most misunderstood thing: a beautiful render is a neural network, and that network has a DNA. Toggle IMAGE / PHENOTYPE / DNA on the same individual to see one thing three ways. That is genotype → phenotype → render, shown rather than lectured.

You, and the swarm

You're part of a live shared world right now. Many machines grow one genealogy together through a PartyServer coordinator: the peer count and the collective gen/s at the top are real, best-per-niche creatures migrate between machines as they're discovered, and the signed tree of life — already everyone's — persists in your browser and grows across visits. Prefer solitude? ?swarm=off keeps you fully local. The coordinator verifies every shared creature's signature and keeps the best per niche; full replication and a zero-knowledge "proof of becoming" for untrusted machines are still to come.

The crowd discovers what one mind alone cannot. A single machine evolving solo is humbled by this loop — v7's self-write made it honestly harder still: the brain writes its DNA from scratch and must discover its own length — and its skill climbs to about ~20% over a couple of thousand generations, still climbing (the emitted length genuinely converging toward the genome's — length-match ≈ 0.9). We stripped the training crutches to keep it honest: a fully teacher-free fitness gets gamed by a degenerate one-gene write, and the plasticity + attention faculties are load-bearing — the brain genuinely reads its self-portrait before it writes. (The clean genotype/phenotype boundary — behaviours are the brain's own output neurons, not DNA channels — costs some skill versus the older entangled loop; the honesty is the point.) The shared search reaches far higher: best-per-niche migration carried earlier worlds' self-encoders far above the lone explorer, and v7's collective ceiling is the fresh world's to write as the islands run it. It's genuinely earned, not a freebie: a blank or random creature scores 0.000, a compact creature can't win for free, and closing more of yourself counts for more. That gap between the lone explorer and the collective is the whole point of doing this together.

What's real vs illustrative

  • Real: the CPPN evolved by NEAT augmenting topologies (add-node / add-connection with innovation numbers, recurrent links) + speciation + textbook innovation-aligned crossover; genuine ES-HyperNEAT (Risi & Stanley) — a quadtree of the CPPN pattern with variance-based division and band-pruning that evolves the placement, density and connectivity of the hidden neurons; an optional Novelty Search mode (Lehman & Stanley); the 3D volumetric render; MAP-Elites quality-diversity (Mouret & Clune); the v7 self-writer loop — the brain takes attention-chosen foveated glimpses of the image it's born in, ponders over recurrent steps with CPPN-painted Hebbian plasticity under Backpropamine-style neuromodulation and halting (ACT), then autoregressively WRITES its DNA from its own neurons, one gene at a time, deciding its own length (seq2seq; all evolved and intrinsic to the one network — no transformer; a continuous cousin of the neural-network quine, Chang & Lipson) — with its honest, complexity-weighted skill (R² on length + values, floor 0.000, length-discovery genuinely evolving); a signed, hash-chained lineage that auto-records the champion line and persists in IndexedDB; and the live swarm — peer count, collective gen/s, and best-per-niche migration through the coordinator.
  • Honest approximations (flagged): the ES-HyperNEAT quadtree resolution is bounded (a max depth) for browser real time — the paper sets a max resolution too, ours is just lower; placement runs on the algorithm's native 2-D sheet while the image is the network's response swept over 3-D space; and per-neuron heterogeneous activations + CPPN-painted biases are Autograph extensions beyond standard ES-HyperNEAT (which uses one activation). All of these are real code, named here so nothing is over-narrated.
  • The measured limit (the soul): the brain genuinely writes its own DNA and learns to decide its own length (the emitted length evolves toward the genome's — we watch it converge, length-match ≈ 0.9), yet it recovers only part of itself (a single garden ≈ 20%, climbing). Self-knowledge here is real but partial: closing the loop perfectly would mean writing your whole self with nothing left over, and the only creature that does is the empty one. So "life is imperfect self-knowledge" is a measured, structural truth, not only a poem — we reach for our true selves and grasp a fraction; the reaching is the life.
  • Roadmap (clearly future): push the self-write deeper (reconstruct more of the DNA than today's ~fifth — and one day its STRUCTURE, not just its gene values: behavioural-equivalence + developmental self-write, the honest v8 frontier); a single dedicated evolution Web Worker + WASM/SIMD to move the (sequential) search off the render thread for a smoother instrument — a multi-core pool is deliberately not taken (it would change the honest numbers); and the zk "proof of becoming" + full verification of untrusted machines (today the swarm's trust is signed-lineage + rate-limiting + keep-best merge), and the quantum framing — metaphor and lineage only. There are no qubits here.
