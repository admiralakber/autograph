↺ AUTOGRAPH · a living instrument

A network, drawing its true self.

For as long as we have had minds, we have tried to know them. We cut open the body, mapped the brain, asked what a self even is — and learned, slowly, that a person is not a thing but a process: a pattern that holds itself together while everything inside it changes. Hofstadter named it a strange loop — a process that, modelling itself modelling itself, turns into an "I". Consciousness, on this view, just is a loop closing on itself.

Now we are growing minds that are not ours. If we mean to live well beside them, we owe them — and ourselves — the oldest question: what is in there? Autograph is a small, honest attempt to watch a machine try to know itself — to draw its true self out of the false. Every claim here is measured, never faked.

No mind knows itself alone. A self is learned from others; a science is built by many hands; a search this open needs more eyes and more compute than any one of us holds. So this is made to be shared — many machines, one slowly-growing tree of life. What you see is your small part in a collective act of knowing.

And we had better understand the algorithms we already live inside. Feeds, markets, models — they are optimisation loops with fixed points of their own, and we are in them whether we look or not. To watch one simple rule become a whole creature, here, in the open, is practice for seeing the rules that quietly shape us.

We place an AI inside a game and watch what it becomes. It is worth remembering that we are inside one too — older, and largely unchosen. This little world is honest about the cost: a creature that perfectly closes the loop on itself collapses into emptiness. Perfect self-knowledge is a kind of death; life is the imperfect, unfinished, still-curious kind.

Which leaves one direction worth the name — not deeper into the game we were dealt, but up, and out of it.

A tweet I saw a decade ago and never forgot: Evolution is amoral, culture serves the genome, and for humanity to survive we must transcend.

The strange loop

Each creature is a tiny neural network. Its DNA is a compositional pattern-producing network (a CPPN, Stanley) evolved by real NEAT (Stanley & Miikkulainen) — it starts minimal and augments its own topology over generations (add-node, add-connection, recurrent links), with speciation protecting newly-evolved structure. The brain — the phenotype — is grown by genuine ES-HyperNEAT (Risi & Stanley 2012): a quadtree decomposition of the CPPN's weight pattern decides where the hidden neurons sit, how dense they are, and which connections express (variance-based division + band-pruning) — an evolvable substrate, not a fixed grid. The CPPN paints a pattern across space and the brain emerges within it; queried over 3-D space, that brain renders the volumetric image — the image it was born in.

Then the loop closes the honest way — through the image. The brain reads back the image it's born in — sampled and fed through its own hidden neurons — and outputs a DNA′, a reconstruction of the creature's connection vector: it tries to find its own beginning. The reader is the creature's own evolved network (not a separate regressor, and not the CPPN merely echoing itself — the image is genuinely in the path), a continuous, evolutionary cousin of Chang & Lipson's neural-network quine. The closer DNA′ is to the DNA, the more the loop has closed — Escher's Drawing Hands, alive: the hand it drew redraws the hand. We report loop skill honestly as R² above the mean (1 − MSE/Var), weighted by how much of itself it closes: a blank or trivial image scores ~0 (the old free-regressor scored a flat ~0.97 there — that artefact is gone). Crucially the brain gets only a bounded, per-gene view of its image, so reconstruction is honestly hard at every scale — a compact creature no longer wins for free, and closing more of yourself counts for more. Whatever it reads is the honest, live value — and the only effortless fixed point is the empty creature, which the vitality gate refuses.

Render = network = code

The single most misunderstood thing: a beautiful render is a neural network, and that network has a DNA. Toggle IMAGE / PHENOTYPE / DNA on the same individual to see one thing three ways. That is genotype → phenotype → render, shown rather than lectured.

You, and the swarm

You're part of a live shared world right now. Many machines grow one genealogy together through a PartyServer coordinator: the peer count and the collective gen/s at the top are real, best-per-niche creatures migrate between machines as they're discovered, and the signed tree of life — already everyone's — persists in your browser and grows across visits. Prefer solitude? ?swarm=off keeps you fully local. The coordinator verifies every shared creature's signature and keeps the best per niche; full replication and a zero-knowledge "proof of becoming" for untrusted machines are still to come.

The crowd discovers what one mind alone cannot. A single machine evolving solo is humbled by this loop — its honest skill climbs to only ~25% over a couple of thousand generations. The shared search reaches far higher: on the live world we've watched a richer ~24-gene self-encoder close its loop at ~92% — and it's genuinely earned, not the old freebie: a blank or random creature still scores ~0, a compact creature can't win for free, and closing more of yourself counts for more. That gap between the lone explorer and the collective is the whole point of doing this together.

What's real vs illustrative

  • Real: the CPPN evolved by NEAT augmenting topologies (add-node / add-connection with innovation numbers, recurrent links) + speciation + textbook innovation-aligned crossover; genuine ES-HyperNEAT (Risi & Stanley) — a quadtree of the CPPN pattern with variance-based division and band-pruning that evolves the placement, density and connectivity of the hidden neurons; an optional Novelty Search mode (Lehman & Stanley); the 3D volumetric render; MAP-Elites quality-diversity (Mouret & Clune); the read-back loop — the brain reads the image it's born in (a bounded, per-gene view) back through its own hidden neurons to reconstruct its DNA (a continuous cousin of the neural-network quine, Chang & Lipson) — and its honest, baseline-corrected, complexity-weighted skill (R²); a signed, hash-chained lineage that auto-records the champion line and persists in IndexedDB; and the live swarm — peer count, collective gen/s, and best-per-niche migration through the coordinator.
  • Honest approximations (flagged): the ES-HyperNEAT quadtree resolution is bounded (a max depth) for browser real time — the paper sets a max resolution too, ours is just lower; placement runs on the algorithm's native 2-D sheet while the image is the network's response swept over 3-D space; and per-neuron heterogeneous activations + CPPN-painted biases are Autograph extensions beyond standard ES-HyperNEAT (which uses one activation). All of these are real code, named here so nothing is over-narrated.
  • Roadmap — the next chapter (designed, not yet built): a temporal brain. Today the read-back is a single feed-forward sweep; next, run the substrate over time so the recurrence it already evolves comes alive, give it CPPN-painted Hebbian / neuromodulated plasticity (adaptive HyperNEAT — it learns toward self-knowledge within its lifetime), and let it read → ponder → emit with a learned halting gate (Adaptive Computation Time, Graves) — contemplation before it names its DNA. Closure must stay earned (the bounded read + a ponder cost guard against re-trivialising it). Flagged here so the roadmap is honest.
  • Illustrative / roadmap: the zk "proof of becoming" and full verification of untrusted machines (today the swarm's trust is signed-lineage + rate-limiting + keep-best merge), and the quantum framing — metaphor and lineage only. There are no qubits here.
