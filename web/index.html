↺ AUTOGRAPH · a living instrument

A network, drawing its true self.

✎ FIRST DRAFT — placeholder words, for Aqeel to make his own.

For as long as we have had minds, we have tried to know them. We cut open the body, mapped the brain, asked what a self even is — and learned, slowly, that a person is not a thing but a process: a pattern that holds itself together while everything inside it changes.

Now we are growing minds that are not ours. If we mean to live well beside them, we owe them — and ourselves — the oldest question: what is in there? Autograph is a small, honest attempt to watch a machine try to know itself — to draw its true self out of the false. Every claim here is measured, never faked.

No mind knows itself alone. A self is learned from others; a science is built by many hands; a search this open needs more eyes and more compute than any one of us holds. So this is made to be shared — many machines, one slowly-growing tree of life. What you see is your small part in a collective act of knowing.

And we had better understand the algorithms we already live inside. Feeds, markets, models — they are optimisation loops with fixed points of their own, and we are in them whether we look or not. To watch one simple rule become a whole creature, here, in the open, is practice for seeing the rules that quietly shape us.

We place an AI inside a game and watch what it becomes. It is worth remembering that we are inside one too — older, and largely unchosen. This little world is honest about the cost: a creature that perfectly closes the loop on itself collapses into emptiness. Perfect self-knowledge is a kind of death; life is the imperfect, unfinished, still-curious kind.

Which leaves one direction worth the name — not deeper into the game we were dealt, but up, and out of it.

Evolution is amoral, culture serves the genome, and for humanity to survive we must transcend. We must… transcend.

What am I looking at?

Tiny neural creatures evolving, live, to draw a picture of themselves. Here are the parts:

🧬 DNA
The genotype — a small CPPN network. It grows by real NEAT: starts minimal, then evolution adds nodes & connections (watch the DNA readout climb).
🧠 BRAIN (phenotype)
A second network whose wiring the DNA paints (HyperNEAT). Signal flows input→output; queried over space it produces the picture.
✨ SELF-PORTRAIT
The actual output — the density & colour the brain draws across 3-D space. The glowing volume is the network.
↺ THE LOOP
Read the self-portrait back into a DNA′ and compare. Loop fidelity is how well a creature re-encodes itself — measured live, never faked.
▦ DIVERSITY MAP
A MAP-Elites grid: one champion per kind (complexity → across, symmetry ↑ up). Brightness = fidelity. The bold white border is the creature you're watching.
🌳 TREE OF LIFE
Every champion is signed & hash-chained into a genealogy that grows on its own and persists across visits.
⚙ CONTROLS
STACKED shows all three at once (tap one to open). FOLLOW BEST tracks the finest self-encoder. TURBO evolves faster. NOVELTY rewards being different, not just faithful (Lehman & Stanley). KEEP signs a creature into your tree.

AUTOGRAPH — the manual

These are lifeforms trying to draw their true self out of the false. You're lending compute to the search. What you're watching is real evolution; where something is illustrative rather than proven, we say so.

The strange loop

Each creature is a tiny neural network. Its DNA is a compositional pattern-producing network (a CPPN) evolved by real NEAT — it starts minimal and augments its own topology over generations (add-node, add-connection, recurrent links), with speciation protecting newly-evolved structure. In HyperNEAT the DNA paints the weights of a second network — the phenotype, the brain — and (simplified ES-HyperNEAT) it also places that brain's neurons. The phenotype, read over 3D space, draws a volumetric self-portrait. Read the self-portrait back at known points and you recover a DNA′. The closer DNA′ is to the DNA, the more the loop has closed — Escher's Drawing Hands, alive. Loop fidelity is measured live and never faked.

Render = network = code

The single most misunderstood thing: a beautiful render is a neural network, and that network has a DNA. Toggle SELF-PORTRAIT / PHENOTYPE / DNA on the same individual to see one thing three ways. That is genotype → phenotype → render, shown rather than lectured.

You, and the swarm

Today this runs entirely on your device — a single local node, no backend, no telemetry. The roadmap is a shared world: many machines growing one genealogy, coordinated by a sandboxed PartyServer coordinator. The signed tree of life persists in your browser and grows across visits; one day it becomes everyone's.

What's real vs illustrative

  • Real: the CPPN evolved by NEAT augmenting topologies (add-node / add-connection with innovation numbers, recurrent links) + speciation, the HyperNEAT substrate with simplified ES-HyperNEAT placement, an optional Novelty Search mode (Lehman & Stanley), the 3D volumetric render, MAP-Elites quality-diversity, loop fidelity (measured), and a signed, hash-chained lineage that auto-records the champion line and persists in IndexedDB.
  • Illustrative / roadmap: the worldwide swarm (single device for now), zk "proof of becoming", full quadtree ES-HyperNEAT (we ship a simplified placement), and the quantum framing — metaphor and lineage only. There are no qubits here.
